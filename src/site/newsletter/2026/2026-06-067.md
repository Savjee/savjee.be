## 🤓 Cool Stuff I Found on the Internet

### [Following strangers](https://www.psypost.org/scientists-just-revealed-a-strange-quirk-in-how-we-exit-train-stations/)
Scientists tracked 100,000 people leaving a Dutch train station and found something oddly familiar: we tend to follow whoever stepped off right in front of us. Even if they're a total stranger, and even if they take a longer path. One person's choice can snowball into an "avalanche" where dozens of people behind them copy the same route.


### [Reprogramming matter](https://news.mit.edu/2026/researchers-reprogram-materials-quickly-rearranging-their-atoms-0513)
MIT researchers figured out how to restructure atoms of a crystal using a precisely aimed electron beam. In about 40 minutes, they created over 40,000 tiny atomic defects in a semiconductor. This technology could help us build extremely sensitive sensors, denser computer storage systems, and more.


### [Tool using bees](https://www.theguardian.com/environment/2026/jun/04/bees-use-tools-to-solve-problems-study-finds)
Scientists discovered bumblebees can use tools to solve problems. Researchers created a bee version of the classic “box-and-banana” test. To get a sugar reward, a bee had to roll a ball to a specific spot and climb on it. This finding suggests that even tiny brains can generate flexible solutions to novel problems. It pushes back against the idea that insects are just simple machines.


### [Retro-tech parenting](https://havenweb.org/2026/05/28/retro-tech.html)
This parent is not banning his kids from using screens, but is instead bringing back CDs, a kitchen landline, and a family computer with a strict whitelist. The argument? Old tech gives kids real independence while parents keep tight control over what actually enters the house. It's very similar to [my NFC movie library]({% link collections.posts, "2023-11-14-how-i-built-an-nfc-movie-library-for-my-kids" %})!

### [Hollywood's Secret Reader](https://lithub.com/the-man-who-reads-books-for-a-living-one-every-two-days/)
Clarke Speicher’s job is to read one novel every two days and write a report on whether they’d work as films. His reports tell directors which elements are cinematic and whether they should “pass” or “consider” the project.

## 👽 Space
### [The leaky ISS](https://arstechnica.com/space/2026/06/work-on-russias-leaky-space-station-module-causes-astronauts-to-take-shelter/)
There's been air leaking from the ISS since 2019 and it slowly got worse. Recently, 5 crew members had to shelter in a SpaceX Dragon capsule for 90 minutes while Russian cosmonauts repaired a leaky transfer tunnel. I guess nobody brought duct tape?


### [AI in space, a bad idea?](https://spectrum.ieee.org/orbital-data-centers-heat)
Everyone’s racing to put AI chips in orbit because space offers unlimited solar power and “free” cooling. However, the vacuum of space is the worse possible heat sink. So why are many companies pursuing it anyway?


### [Why not Venus?](https://mceglowski.substack.com/p/why-not-venus)
Maciej Cegłowski argues we shouldn’t be fixated on Mars and instead look toward Venus. It’s closer to Earth, has an atmosphere that can shield us from radiation, and there might be life in the clouds. The only downside is: we’d have to live in floating air balloons because the pressure on ground level would instantly crush us.


## 🏥 Health & Medicine

### [AI-designed vaccine](https://interestingengineering.com/ai-robotics/cambridge-ai-designed-universal-vaccine-phase-1-trial)
Researchers at Cambridge used AI to design a "super-antigen" vaccine meant to protect against whole families of coronaviruses. This antigen targets shared weaknesses across many related viruses and could allow us to create vaccines proactively rather than reactively.


### [Google wants to "debug" California](https://www.theguardian.com/technology/2026/jun/01/google-permission-release-mosquitoes-california-florida)
Google is asking the EPA to release up to 32 million sterilized male mosquitoes in California and Florida over two years. The idea behind its Debug program is simple: flood neighborhoods with sterile males to reduce the total population. This method has already been used in Singapore where it led to an 80-90% reduction in the mosquito population and a 70% drop in dengue cases.


### [Ancient yeast](https://uk.news.yahoo.com/scientists-yeast-ancient-icemans-guts-002754866.html?ncid=redditnewsus)
Scientists studying Ötzi the Iceman found cold-loving yeast still living in his gut, skin, and meltwater. So what do you do with those? Spend three months creating sourdough bread with it, of course!


### [Fully simulated fruit fly](https://mezha.ua/en/news/the-first-fly-brain-emulation-309245/)
Scientists at Eon Systems took a full wiring map of a fruit fly brain and built a digital version of it. They then connected this digital brain to a virtual environment and discovered that their digital actually fly came alive! This is getting close to scanning a brain and uploading it into a computer.


## ⚡️ Energy & Environment

### [A million vapes a day](https://www.theguardian.com/society/2026/may/17/disposable-vapes-suez-uk)
The UK banned single-use vapes a year ago, but around 6.3 million devices still hit the bin every week. And that’s a problem, because vapes caused over 80% of fires at UK recycling sites last year.


### [Inside a BYD](https://www.lumafield.com/scan-of-the-month/byd)
Lumafield ran four BYD parts through an industrial CT scanner: a battery cell, a door switch panel, a portable charger, and a key fob. The scans can catch the smallest imperfections, like slightly uneven battery windings.


### [Super El Niño is coming](https://www.nationalgeographic.com/environment/article/super-el-nino-extreme-weather-climate)
Forecasters think we’re heading into a strong El Niño this year. It means large parts of the Pacific get unusually warm and start affecting weather patterns around the world. Some places will face extreme droughts, while others will face floods.
